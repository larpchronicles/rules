# How Alive Are You, Really?

## Body and Armor
Your character's survivability is measured by two values: **Body** and **Armor**.

### Body

Body represents your character's physical health. All characters start with **10 Body** and can gain more through the Toughness skill.

**Current Body** is the amount of Body your character has right now. **Maximum Body** is the highest value to which your character can be healed. **Temporary Body** is Body that exists in excess of your Maximum Body, granted by certain effects. Temporary Body is always lost before regular Body, and once lost, it cannot be healed or recovered.

When you take damage, it reduces your Current Body. Regardless of how much damage you take, your Body can never drop below -1.

### Armor

Armor provides an additional layer of protection on top of Body. Damage is always deducted from Armor first, then from Body once Armor is depleted. 

There are two sources of Armor:

**Physical Armor** is actual armor that your character — and you! — wear. Physical armor must be represented by real armor or a convincing costume piece (a physrep) and is typically made from leather, chain, plate, or similar materials (See [[Armor Phys Reps]]). When you check in, your armor phys rep will be assessed and assigned an Armor Value. The Armor Value represents the highest value armor tag that this phys rep can represent.

**Dexterity Armor** represents your character's agility and ability to avoid or deflect blows. It costs more Skill Points for the same amount of armor as physical armor, but requires no phys rep as it is an innate quality of your character.

Physical Armor and Dexterity Armor do not stack. You may only benefit from one at a time. Upon refitting a suit of armor, all other sources of armor you may posses are reduced to zero.

#### Refitting Armor [!exclude-toc]
**Refitting Armor** restores your armor to its full value. Refitting Physical Armor requires 1 minute of concentration, spent roleplaying adjusting straps, pushing out dents, tightening buckles. Refitting Dexterity Armor also requires 1 minute, roleplayed as stretching, shadowboxing, or similar physical conditioning. 

A character may not walk or use any skills during a refit. If the refit is interrupted (by taking damage, being affected by an ability, or any other game interaction), it must be restarted from the beginning.

## Defenses

Defenses allow your character to negate or avoid incoming attacks. Most defenses are granted by skills, abilities, spells, or items, and each one specifies what types of attacks it can defend against.

### Defense Slots

Every character has **1 Defense Slot** by default, with additional slots available through the Additional Defense Slot skill. Defense Slots limit how many defenses you can have ready at any given time.

A defense must be **prepared** in a Defense Slot before it can be used. Only **Active Defenses** — defenses that are currently prepared in a slot — may be used to defend against attacks. You may have as many Active Defenses as you have Defense Slots.

### Preparing Defenses

To prepare a defense, you speak the preparation verbal associated with the ability. This is typically a short phrase that identifies what you're preparing.

> [!EXAMPLE] Preparation Verbals
> 
> - **Parry**: "I prepare my defenses against Physical."
> - **Resist Magic**: "I prepare my defenses against Magic."
> - **Barrier Flame**: "I call forth mystic force to barrier Flame."

If all of your Defense Slots are full and you wish to prepare a new defense, you must replace one of your current Active Defenses. You choose which one to drop.

Some effects granted by items or plot-specific abilities function as Active Defenses but do not require a Defense Slot. These will always state so explicitly in their description.

### Using Defenses

When you are hit by an attack that one of your Active Defenses applies to, you may call **"Resist"** or **"Dodge"** (as appropriate to the defense) to negate the attack entirely. The defense is then expended and the Defense Slot it occupied is now empty.

If a single attack contains multiple components (such as a weapon blow with a carrier effect), and your defense applies to any part of it, the entire attack is negated.

Active Defenses that have been used can be re-prepared by speaking the preparation verbal again, provided you have the ability or effect available to do so.

All Active Defenses are lost if the character dies and is restored by a Life effect or Revivify. They must be re-prepared after returning to life.


## States of Health 
### How alive are you, really?

Your character's state depends on their Current Body:

#### Conscious
**Conscious** (1+ Body): Your character can act normally. You are able to move, speak, fight, cast spells, use items and abilities.

#### Unconscious
**Unconscious** (0 Body): Your character collapses and is unaware of the world around them. They cannot move, speak, or take any game actions. After 1 minute, the character wakes up with 1 Body. 

#### Bleeding Out
A character reduced below 0 Body drops to **-1 Body** (regardless of how much damage the final blow dealt) and begins **Bleeding Out**. The character is unconscious and dying.

Bleeding Out lasts **1 minute**. During this time:

- Any healing effect will restore the character's Body by the stated amount (calculated from -1) and end the Bleeding Out state. The character regains consciousness immediately.
- **First Aid** will pause the Bleeding Out count. First Aid takes 1 minute of uninterrupted concentration. Once complete, the character is at 0 Body and Unconscious (and will wake with 1 Body after 1 minute). If the person performing First Aid is interrupted, the Bleeding Out count resumes where it left off.

After 1 minute of Dying without healing or First Aid, the character is now Dead.

> [!EXAMPLE] 
> Ulysses the Unlucky is Dying. Thirty seconds in, his friend casts a Cure Light Wounds 10, which heals Ulysses for 10 Body. His Current Body goes from -1 to 9. He is Conscious and back in the fight.

#### Dead
While Dead:

- The character is not aware of their surroundings and cannot take any game actions.
- The character has **5 minutes** to receive a **Life** spell, **Revivify** spell, or other Life effect. If they do, they are restored to life as described by the spell or effect.
- Any temporary spell effects and status effects on the character end when they die, included prepared defenses and buffs. These are not regained if the character receives a Life effect, though new Defenses can be prepared and new buffs applied via the normal rules. Permanent effects and any effects that explicitly persists through death are retained.
 
After 5 minutes of being Dead, the character's body is no longer strong enough to hold onto the spirit. The player announces **"Body dissipates,"** and the character's spirit seeks resurrection.

### Dissipation and Resurrection

The player announces **"Body dissipates,"** drops all in-game items to the ground, puts on a white headband, and goes Out of Game. The player should then find a marshal or head to Logistics to register the death.

The character's spirit departs to seek resurrection at the nearest safe **Resurrection Circle**. Deciding to resurrect at a given Resurrection Circle is an Out of Game decision, a player may not be compelled to resurrect in a circle the player considers to be unsafe for their character.

A player may choose to refuse any mechanical effect by voluntarily dissipating instead of taking the effect. This is solely an Out of Game decision, and thus, a character can never be forced to dissipate in this manner. The character drops all in-game items and the player goes Out of Game to seek resurrection as normal.

> [!NOTE] Regarding Resurrection
> A player may always decline to resurrect at any specific Resurrection Circle. This decision is purely Out of Game on the part of the player, and does not require explanation or justification. Staff will identify alternate locations the character may choose to resurrect at.

When a character's spirit arrives at a Resurrection Circle, the character reforms with full Body, full Armor, and restored skills. All non-ritual, non-permanent effects are removed upon resurrection.

Resurrection incurs **SP Debt**, which is described in the Skills chapter.


### Killing Blow

A Killing Blow is a deliberate finishing strike against a helpless target. To perform a Killing Blow, place a weapon (or your hand) against the target's torso and count: **"Killing Blow 1, Killing Blow 2, Killing Blow 3"** followed by a damage type or spell incant. If the count is completed without interruption, the target becomes Dead.

A Killing Blow's damage type matches whatever the character would normally swing. A barehanded Killing Blow is Normal. A character may also use a spell incant in place of a damage type.

A Killing Blow can be interrupted by any attack that strikes the person performing it. The target of a Killing Blow may refuse it if they are capable of movement — a Killing Blow against a conscious, mobile target is assumed to be automatically refused. A Killing Blow can only succeed against a target that is incapacitated, restrained, or otherwise unable to move, or accepted willingly.

### Carrying a Body

A character that is Unconscious, Bleeding Out, or Dead may be carried by another character. To pick up a body, roleplay lifting them on a three count: **"One I pick you up, two I pick you up, three I pick you up."** Both players must remain within arm's reach of each other, and the carrying player may not move faster than a walk. If any effect forces the carrier to move quickly or prevents them from holding items, they must drop the body on a one count: **"One I drop you."**
