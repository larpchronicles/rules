# Playing the Game

## In Game vs. Out of Game

At a Chronicles event, everything exists in one of two states: **In Game (IG)** or **Out of Game (OOG)**. This applies to locations, players, and items.

When something is In Game, it exists in the world of Evren. You and other characters can see it, interact with it, and be affected by it. When something is Out of Game, it does not exist in the game world. Your character cannot perceive or interact with it, and it cannot affect your character.

### Players: Going In and Out of Game

A player who is In Game is actively portraying their character. A player who is Out of Game is just themselves, their character isn't present. 

To go Out of Game, you should move to a discreet or out-of-the-way location, ask out loud "Does anyone mind if I go Out of Game?", then quietly do a 10-count ("One I walk into the woods, Two I walk into the woods..." etc.) and put on a **white headband** or place your hand (or a held item) on top of your head.

While Out of Game, you are invisible to the game world. No character may interact with you, and you may not interact with any character or game element. You should move out of the way of any active game play if possible, and avoid lingering in areas where your presence might disrupt immersion.

To go In Game requires a 3-count, as appropriate for the situation — "One I come from the forest, Two I come from the forest, Three I come from the forest," or a skeleton might use "One I rise from the ground..." Remove your white headband, proceed with the count, and once completed, you are In Game.

A player may go Out of Game at any time for any reason. No explanation is required for this, though they are expected to move away so as not to disrupt any players remaining In Game.

#### After Player-vs-Player Conflict

After initiating hostile actions against another player's character, such as combat, theft, or other acts of aggression, a player may not go Out of Game for 30 minutes. This ensures that the affected player has the opportunity to respond in game. This rule is enforced in the spirit of fair play. A player who genuinely needs to go Out of Game for personal reasons will not be penalized for doing so, but a pattern of using OOG transitions to avoid the consequences of in-game actions will be addressed by staff.

### Locations: IG and OOG Areas

Nearly the entire site is considered In Game. Your cabin, the tavern, the paths between them, and the surrounding woods are all part of the game world. 

The following areas are always Out of Game:
- **Logistics / NPC Camp**
- **Bathrooms and showers**
- **Parking lots**

Players may not chase or follow another character into an Out of Game area, and may not use an Out of Game areas to hide from In Game events.

Some areas are In Game but designated as **No-Combat zones**. In these areas, your character is present and can interact with others, but combat is not permitted. If combat is imminent, players should call a Hold! and relocate everyone involved to an acceptable In-Game area. Kitchens and other areas with safety concerns are common No-Combat zones. Staff will identify these areas during PC Talk.

### Items: In Game and Out of Game

In Game items are anything with an item tag or that is clearly part of the game world (weapons, armor, coins, potions, etc.). Out of Game items are personal belongings (phones, car keys, wallet, sleeping bag, medications, etc.).

Keep these separate. Store OOG items under your bed or in a container marked "OOG." In Game items should never be stored in a way that makes them inaccessible to the game world (such as locked in your car or hidden in an OOG container). Items that are brought into game cannot later be stored in an OOG area, and any items kept in an OOG area cannot be brought In Game. 

### Headbands
#### Orange Headbands - Non Combatants & Pages
Pages are people that are unable to enter combat. They may be children or adults, although any child under age 14 must be a Page. Pages may not use combat skills, weapons, or throw packets.

**You may not ever physically strike a Page.** If you engage in combat with a page, you may point your weapon or packet and say, "Page, I kill you." They cannot participate in combat. They are treated as if they have 1 body.

#### White Headbands - Out of Game

Once you arrive on site, you are entering an In Game Area. If you are not in game as your character yet, you must have a white headband on. If you do not have a white headband, you may request one from staff. If there are none available, you may signify your out of game status by holding a hand to your forehead or touching the top of your head with a held item. This gesture is accepted as an out of character signal and frequently used by NPCs when not in game.

If you are in game, you should not react or speak to anyone that is wearing a white headband without first going out of game yourself. It is generally seen as poor etiquette to break the environment for others.

When your character dies and your dying count has expired, after dropping all in game items, you should put on a white headband and go to Logistics/NPC Camp to register your death. Your spirit cannot interact in any manner with others.
