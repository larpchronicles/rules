# What is Chronicles?
Chronicles is a Live Action Roleplaying Game (LARP) set in a fantasy world called Evren. Over the course of a weekend event, you will portray a character of your own creation: an adventurer arriving at a market faire in the port city of Varos, where swords, sorcery, and exotic races are commonplace. You decide who your character is, what they want, and how they act. The stories that unfold are shaped by the Plot team (similar to Game Masters in a tabletop roleplaying game), your choices and the choices of every other player at the event.

LARP is improvisational theater meets tabletop RPG meets renaissance faire. Everyone is in costume. Orcs are actually green (face paint). Elves actually have pointed ears (simple prosthetics). Instead of saying what your character is going to do, you must actually do it. Combat is resolved with foam weapons, and magic is cast by speaking an incant and throwing a packet at your target. Traps are real (albeit harmless) mechanisms, doors have complex locking mechanisms. The world exists around you, physically, and you move through it as your character.

A typical Chronicles event runs from Friday evening to Sunday morning at a Boy Scout camp or similar wooded, secluded site. You'll sleep in cabins, eat meals in a tavern, and spend the time in between adventuring: which might mean fighting goblins in the woods, negotiating a trade agreement with a visiting noble, investigating a mystery that's been unfolding over several events, or piecing together clues to determine where the long lost artifact might be found.

Your character grows by attending events, earning Skill Points that you spend to acquire new skills, spells, and abilities. There are no rigid classes; instead, you choose primary and secondary skillsets that determine the cost of different skill categories, allowing you to build anything from a sword-and-board fighter to a wandering healer to a spellcasting locksmith. The system rewards attending events as a player or a volunteer; there is no "Pay-to-Win" shortcut.

## Who Is This Book For?

This rulebook is written for everyone: new players who have never held a boffer weapon and hardened LARP veterans alike. If you're new, start with the next section, **Your First Market Faire**, which walks you through an entire weekend event in narrative form. 

You don't need to memorize the whole rulebook before your first event; a general concept of the system at a high level and a willingness to learn as you go are all that's needed.

If you're experienced with Chronicles or another LARP system, the Table of Contents will get you where you need to go. The rules chapters are organized for reference: character creation and skills in Part 1, combat and game mechanics in Part 2, and advanced topics like ritual magic and advanced transformations in Part 5.

## How to Get Involved

There are several ways to get started, and you should move at whatever speed is comfortable for you:

- **Ask questions on Discord.** Our community is active and happy to help: larpchronicles.com/discord
- **Make a character and attend an event.** The walkthrough in this book and the character creator at larpchronicles.com will get you there.
- **NPC for a weekend.** Volunteering as a Non-Player Character is a great way to experience different aspects of the game — you'll try multiple combat styles, use a variety of abilities, and have staff on hand to guide you through the rules. And you'll earn Skill Points for a character of your own while you do it.
- **Come hang out before a game.** Site opens early. Meet people, ask questions, get a feel for the community before you commit to a full weekend.

Whatever path you choose, we look forward to seeing you at the next market faire!
