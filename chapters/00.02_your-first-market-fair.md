# Your First Market Fair
You've made a character, you've packed the car, and you're pulling into a campsite on a Friday evening. You park in the parking lot with the other cars, and you can see a few people hauling bins, foam weapons, and packs from their cars to cabins. Someone waves at you. You have no idea what you're doing. That's fine; almost everyone felt this way their first time.

Here's what the rest of the weekend will look like.

## Arrival and Check-In 
The site opens around 5:00 PM, check-in begins about 7:00 PM. Find the building marked **Logistics** or **NPC Camp**. It is the out-of-game hub where staff operates for the weekend. This is where you check in. 

Check-in is straightforward. At the logistics desk, a staff member will verify your registration, have you sign a liability waiver and code of conduct if you haven't already, confirm your event fee, go over your character sheet, and run a safety check on any weapons or shields you brought. If you don't have your own weapons yet, that's okay! NPC Camp can lend you what you'll need to get you through the weekend. 

Once you're checked in, find your cabin, claim a bunk, and get into costume. Stash any obviously modern items and other out-of-game items (phones, car keys, duffel bags) under your bed (or labeled "OOG") where they won't disrupt the immersive atmosphere. Put on a **white headband** before you head out. The white headband tells everyone that you're out-of-game: your character isn't here yet. You're invisible. Nobody in-game will interact with you while you're wearing it.

## PC Talk

Before the game starts, everyone gathers in the building typically used for the **tavern** (where food will be served, and the typical hangout for PCs throughout the weekend) for **PC Talk**. This is an out-of-game briefing where staff covers important information for the weekend: safety reminders, any relevant rule updates, and a few things your characters would know going into the market fair. It's short, and it ends with a specific time announced for **Lay On** (about 9:00 PM), which is the moment the game officially begins.

When Lay On is called, if you are ready, take off your white headband: you are now your character.

## Friday Night: Your First Steps In-Game
The tavern is alive. People are talking, laughing, reminiscing, or catching up with old friends. An elf at a corner table is reading a book by candlelight. A human is haggling with a dwarf about getting a new sword forged. A few armed figures huddle around a map, discussing how they're going to go about dealing with an orc warband.

This is the market fair in the port city of Varos, and your character has just arrived.

Nobody expects you to know everything. Many people in the room have been playing for months or years, and almost all of them remember what it felt like to walk in for the first time. Introduce your character. Ask questions in character, if you can. If you have a question that doesn't fit in game, that's completely fine, too. Simply lower your voice or ask off to the side.

Putting a hand or weapon on top of your head is the universal signal for "I'm out of character," and you can use it anytime you need to be out of game for more than a couple moments, or to make clear that a question is purely out of game. 

A good opening move: find the tavern, introduce yourself, ask about what's going on and if anyone needs any help with anything. You'll be surprised at how quickly you get pulled into something.

## Friday Bedtime
Whenever you're ready for bed, head back to your cabin. Keep your in-game belongings such as item tags, coin pouches, weapons separate from your personal stuff. Out-of-game items stored under your bed or in a bag marked "OOG" won't be touched, even if someone's character breaks into your cabin overnight (yes, that can happen, but **only** with a designated staff member called a "Rogue Marshal" present, and they'll never touch your out-of-game belongings).

Showers and washrooms are always out-of-game spaces. Nobody will ambush you on the way to the bathroom, and you can go out-of-game for the walk there and back by putting on a white headband or putting your hand on top of your head.

Your cabin itself is in-game; it's where your character is staying for the weekend. If you want to sleep without being disturbed, you can go out-of-game by taping an OOG sign on the foot of your bed or above the bunk. Just know that if your character is holding onto something that half the market fair is looking for, a marshal _might_ come knocking. That's rare on a first event, but it's worth knowing the convention: the game world keeps moving while you sleep, even if your character is resting safely behind a locked door.

Don't be surprised if you hear combat outside your cabin late at night or early in the morning. That's normal. Decide if you're going to go out and help the other adventurers, or roll over and go back to sleep; it's entirely up to you! Though some of the best stories start with "I heard yelling outside my cabin and grabbed my sword..."

## Saturday Morning [!toc-exclude-children-only]
Saturday is where the game really opens up. Breakfast is served in the tavern. People are making plans. Someone has heard about a cave of goblins that have been bothering local farmers. A merchant is looking for some adventurers to deal with some bandits on the road. At NPC Camp, staff has put up the Mod Request Box, where players can indicate their desire for specific adventures.

### Your First Mod
At some point, maybe because you requested a specific adventure, maybe because someone in the tavern invited you along, or maybe because a wild-eyed farmer burst through the door begging for help, you'll head out on your first **mod**.

A mod is a short adventure run by staff. A **Plot Marshal** and **NPCs** (Non-Player Characters) will lead your group away from the in-town area near the tavern, describe the scene, and referee anything that comes up. Mods might involve combat, negotiation, puzzle-solving, scouting, or all of the above. Not every mod is a fight, but many involve some danger.

Let's say your group has agreed to investigate the cave. The marshal walks you to a wide forest path, describes the squeaks and grunts coming from within a rocky outcropping, and tells you there's a faint light flickering from deep inside. The marshal will ask if everyone is ready, then calls "3, 2, 1 Lay On!" Your group moves in.

### Your First Combat
You hear rustling. Three figures in dark tabards emerge from behind trees, weapons raised. They're the NPCs, volunteer players portraying the people and monsters the story calls for. This time, they're bandits.

Here's what happens next:

One of them swings a foam sword at the fighter next to you and calls out **"3 Normal!"** That's a damage call: 3 points of Normal damage. The fighter blocks it with her shield, so the damage is negated. She swings back: **"5 Normal!"** Her sword connects with the bandit's arm. He takes the damage.

Another bandit throws a small cloth bundle filled with birdseed, called a packet, at someone behind you and shouts **"I command you to be stunned!"** That's an ability verbal. The packet hits your friend's shoulder. He's stunned: he can't move, can't attack, can't use abilities. He just has to stand there until it wears off in 60 seconds, or someone else removes the effect.

The third bandit is heading for you.

You've got a short sword, and your character sheet says you swing for 2 Normal damage. You call **"2 Normal!"** and swing, aiming for light contact, like you're playing tag, not trying to hurt anyone. Your foam sword taps the bandit's torso. He acknowledges that he took the damage by saying, "Got it". He swings back, announcing **"3 Normal"** as he does so. You try to block with your short sword, but don't get there in time to stop his sword from hitting your forearm. You had 10 Body, so taking the 3 damage leaves you at 7 Body. At -1 Body, you'll be dead... but that's later.

Here and now, you hear someone shout, **"I conjure a mana bolt 10!"** from behind you. A packet sails over your shoulder and hits the bandit in the chest. He drops to his knees and announces **"Down!"** Your group's Arcane mage is pulling his weight.

**If you didn't catch a call, that's okay.** Just ask. "What was that?" or "Can you repeat that?" are completely normal things to say, even mid-combat. Everyone needs to ask "What hit me?" and "What does that do?" sometimes, even veteran players. 

### Getting Healed
You took 3 points of damage in that fight. You're at 7 out of 10 Body. To get back to full, you'll need a spell or a potion. Luckily, one of your compatriots is a Spirit Mage. They tap you on the shoulder and ask, "First Aid: How much body are you down?" This is indicative of their character triaging you and assessing your wounds. Strictly speaking, they could have healed you without checking first, but this way they know which spell to cast so as to not waste their mana with higher level spells. You respond with, "3," and the mage taps your shoulder again with a packet and incants, **"I call upon the spirits to heal light wounds 10."** And just like that, you're back to full Body! _(You can't exceed your maximum Body, so the excess is lost, leaving you at 10 Body.)_

If you had been wearing armor, you could **refit** your armor, restoring its armor value, by spending 60 seconds roleplaying adjusting and repairing your armor. Unfortunately, you left your armor back in the cabin! Next time, though...

### But What If My Character Dies?
It happens to everyone, eventually. Maybe a mod went sideways, or you got ambushed on the way back to your cabin. Regardless, here's what'll happen:
- Once you go below 0 Body, you'll fall down to the ground (safely!) and your character's unconscious body will lie there limp...
- Your character is **Bleeding Out**. You've got 60 seconds for someone to stabilize you with First Aid, or heal you with a spell or potion. If they do, great! Get back in the fight! If not...
- After 60 seconds of **Bleeding Out**, your character is now **Dead**. Healing won't work any more, you need a **Life** spell or a **Revivify** spell. You've got 5 minutes for someone to cast one on you. If they don't...
- Your character's body dissipates and needs to seek resurrection. You'll drop all your in-game items, go out of game, and speak with a staff member. They'll record the death on your character sheet and tell you where the Healer's Guild is, which is where your character's body will reform.

### Searching and Loot
When you defeat enemies, you may search them by approaching them and saying **"I search you."** The NPC will hand over whatever game items they're carrying, usually coins or item tags. On mods, however, NPCs won't wait for you to search them. Instead, after the end of the mod, the plot marshal will give the players a box containing the loot that their characters found on the mod, to be distributed fairly amongst the mod participants. This helps keep the mod rolling, since the NPCs are needed to play the next group of enemies that the PCs will encounter.

### After the Mod
Once the bandits have been defeated and you have rescued the merchant's goods, your group will head back to town. Mark off the expendable skills you used during the mod on your battleboard, a form you'll have received with your character sheet that helps track your ability use. 

In the tavern, whoever collected the treasure at the end will sit down and split the loot into a pile for each character as fairly as possible. In the event that this isn't possible (such as a singular magic item or other special case), the group will collectively decide what the best choice is. It could be a random lottery, the item might be auctioned off and the proceeds split amongst the rest of the group, or unanimous decision to give it to the new adventurer (lucky you!). Regardless, it should always be handled fairly. _(Like just about everything, if something doesn't feel right to you, talk to a staff member!)_

### Resting
If you are low on mana or abilities, you may choose to Rest. Doing so will give you all of your used abilities and mana points back to full. To Rest, your character spends about 10 minutes recovering and recuperating. During this time, you just go to Logistics to have your battleboard signed off by a staff member and pick up a fresh one. And just like that, your character is freshly rested and ready to go on another adventure!

## The Rest of Saturday
Aside from lunch being served (and later, dinner), the rest of Saturday will continue with mods and NPCs coming into town to attack or roleplay with the adventurers. Around or shortly after dinner, there is frequently a larger assault on the town or a higher stakes mod that involves all of the players at the event. Afterwards, mods and in-town encounters will continue until late into night.

## Sunday Morning
Around 3-4 AM Sunday morning, everyone will have gone to sleep, and the event will be over. When you wake up on Sunday, you'll pack up your belongings, load up your car, and clean your cabin: take the trash out, sweep the floor, make sure the lights and such are turned off. Being respectful of the camp is how we make sure we can come back for the next event!

You'll take your character sheet, any coins you'd like to deposit into your bank, and any temporary item tags you've acquired to Logistics, where you'll find the check-out station. You'll place all this into a designated envelope with your name, so staff can record any changes from the event and you can get your Skill Points for attending the event. 

At the check-out station will be a QR code and link that will take you to the post-event survey and feedback, which aren't required but are greatly appreciated.

Then, if you have the time and you'd like some Goblin Points (which can be exchanged for a number of different things, like free events and meals), you could help the staff pack up NPC camp and get it loaded. It cannot be expressed how much the staff appreciate this!
